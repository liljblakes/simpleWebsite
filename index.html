What I Stand For

My values are the “rules I live by” when I make decisions—especially when no one is watching. They shape my relationships, my work ethic, and how I handle pressure.

My Top Four Values

1) Integrity

What it means: Doing the right thing even when it’s inconvenient.

How I live it: Being honest about my work, owning mistakes fast, and keeping my word.

2) Discipline

What it means: Consistency over motivation—showing up and putting in the reps.

How I live it: Sticking to routines, training, and focusing on long-term goals.

3) Curiosity & Growth

What it means: Always learning, asking better questions, and improving.

How I live it: Taking feedback, building skills, and not staying comfortable.

4) Kindness & Respect

What it means: Treating people well—especially when it’s easy to be dismissive.

How I live it: Listening first, assuming good intent, and being a steady presence.

Where These Values Come From

  • Family & upbringing: Seeing how trust is built (or broken) through daily actions.
  • Sports & fitness: Discipline taught me that results follow consistency.
  • School & career goals: Integrity matters in business—reputation is everything.
  • Real life pressure: Stress reveals character; values keep me grounded.

Why These Values Matter to Me

These values help me stay focused and confident in my decisions. They act like a filter: if a choice hurts my integrity, weakens my discipline, stops my growth, or disrespects others, it’s not worth it—even if it looks like a shortcut.

What Happens If I Don’t Adhere to These Values

Integrity

If I compromise integrity, I lose trust—once trust is gone, relationships and opportunities shrink fast.

Discipline

If I stop being disciplined, I become inconsistent, and my goals turn into “ideas” instead of outcomes.

Curiosity & Growth

If I stop learning, I get comfortable and fall behind—my potential stays unused.

Kindness & Respect

If I lose respect for others, I damage connections and become someone I wouldn’t want to be around.
